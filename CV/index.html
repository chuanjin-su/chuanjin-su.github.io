Research & Projects

  • Thermomass theory and its application [1], Tsinghua University
    2020 – 2022
    • Studied the dynamical behavior of non-Fourier heat transfer by applying the relativity theory, and developed the general heat conduction equation macroscopically.
    • Showed that non-Fourier terms of heat conduction result from relativistic effects, and the nature of the Fourier’s law is the balance between driven force and resistance.
  • Entransy theory and the optimization of heat transfer [2], Tsinghua University
    2020 – 2022
    • Formulated a general model for parabolic transport, and studied the irreversibility, stability, and the optimization of such systems.
    • Demonstrated that the entropy production rate cannot be used as the Lyapunov function for non-equilibrium processes.
  • Ideal dense matter equation of state [3], Tsinghua University
    2021 – 2022
    • Performed a molecular dynamics (MD) simulation of the ideal dense matter to verify its equation of state (EOS) that is proposed based on symmetric relations between ideal dense matter and ideal gas.
    • Proved that internal energy and work capacity of ideal dense matter are independent of temperature. It is also found that the accuracy of ideal dense matter EOS in describing thermodynamic properties is positively related to the density.
  • ANEO High-Performance Computing Project, CentraleSupélec
    Mars 2020 – April 2020
    • Developed a genetic algorithm to optimally schedule interdependent computing tasks for high-performance computing in collaboration with ANEO.
    • A large-scale test of scheduling 500 000 tasks proved the validation of the developed algorithm with a loss of performance (~10%).
  • AFM Project, CentraleSupélec
    Feb 2020 – Jun 2020
    • To build a low-budget Atomic Force Microscope (AFM) by using an optical pick-up unit (OPU) and a piezoelectric plate. Supervised by Prof. Pascal Morenton.
